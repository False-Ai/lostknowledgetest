Community Guidelines

How we keep the archive accurate, respectful, and valuable for everyone.

Lost Knowledge is built on trust. Every Keeper who contributes does so in the spirit of genuine preservation. These guidelines exist not to restrict you, but to ensure everything in this archive is worth keeping.

✦ What we expect from every Keeper

🎯
Be accurate

Only submit knowledge you have genuine reason to believe is accurate. Cite sources where possible. If you are unsure about something, say so in the body of your entry. Deliberately false entries will be rejected and may result in your account being suspended.

📚
Be specific

A title like "Pottery" is too broad. "Korean Onggi Fermentation Vessel Coiling Technique" is an entry. The more specific you are, the more useful your contribution is to future researchers and Keepers.

🌍
Respect all traditions equally

The archive is a space for all of humanity's knowledge — not just Western or well-documented traditions. Treat every culture's practices with the same curiosity and respect you would want shown to your own.

💬
Comment constructively

Comments are for sharing knowledge, pointing out inaccuracies politely, and asking genuine questions. They are not for personal attacks, arguments, or off-topic discussion. Comments that violate this will be removed.

⚖️
Vote honestly

Use Preserve when you genuinely believe the entry is accurate and valuable. Use Dispute only when you have real factual concerns — not simply because you disagree with the topic. Do not create multiple accounts to manipulate votes.

🔒
Keep sensitive knowledge appropriate

Some traditional knowledge involves sacred or restricted practices. Be mindful of communities that consider certain knowledge private. If you are unsure whether to share something, ask whether the knowledge holders would consent to its publication.

✦ Enforcement

First offence
Entry rejected or comment removed with a note explaining why.
Repeat
All entries returned to pending status for re-review.
Serious
Account suspension and permanent removal from the archive.
