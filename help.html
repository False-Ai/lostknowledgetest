Getting Started

What is Lost Knowledge? ▾

Lost Knowledge is a community archive dedicated to preserving dying traditions, forgotten crafts, endangered languages, and ancient wisdom before they vanish from human memory. Anyone can register as a Keeper and contribute entries to the archive.

How do I create an account? ▾

Click Register in the top navigation. Enter a username, email address, and a password (minimum 8 characters, one uppercase letter, one number). Your account is created instantly and you are automatically signed in.

What is a Keeper? ▾

A Keeper is any registered user who contributes to the archive. The name reflects the mission — you are keeping knowledge alive. There is no application process. Simply register and start contributing.

I forgot my password. What do I do? ▾

At the moment, password resets must be handled by the site administrator. Please contact the admin at admin@lostknowledge.local with your registered email address and a reset will be issued.

Submitting Entries

What can I submit? ▾

Any knowledge that is at risk of being lost. This includes dying crafts and artisanal techniques, oral traditions and folklore, herbal and traditional medicine, endangered agricultural practices, vanishing languages and scripts, traditional navigation methods, sacred rituals and ceremonies, and architectural techniques no longer in practice.

How does the review process work? ▾

Every submission goes through admin review before appearing in the public archive. After submitting, your entry will show as Pending in your dashboard. Once approved it becomes Approved and is visible to everyone. Entries that do not meet quality standards are marked Rejected with a note.

Can I edit my entry after submitting? ▾

Yes. Go to your Dashboard, find the entry, and click Edit. After editing, the entry returns to Pending status for re-review. This ensures the archive maintains accuracy.

What makes a good entry? ▾

Good entries follow these principles:

  • Specific title — describe exactly what the knowledge is, not just a broad topic.
  • Clear summary — one sentence that explains what the knowledge is and why it matters.
  • Detailed body — explain the practice in full. How was it done? Who practiced it? Why did it disappear?
  • Accurate region and era — helps researchers and future Keepers find your entry.

Voting & Comments

What does Preserve vs Dispute mean? ▾

▲ Preserve means you believe this knowledge is valuable and accurate — vote this if you want to see it stay in the archive. ▼ Dispute means you have factual concerns or believe the entry contains inaccuracies. Voting is anonymous and can be changed at any time.

Do I need an account to vote? ▾

Yes. You must be signed in to vote or comment. This prevents duplicate votes and keeps the archive trustworthy. Register for free to participate.

Can I delete my own comment? ▾

Yes. A delete button appears next to comments you have posted. Admins can also delete any comment that violates community guidelines.

Your Account

What is the dashboard? ▾

Your dashboard shows all your submitted entries, their current status (Pending / Approved / Rejected), the votes each received, and your saved bookmarks. You can also manage and delete entries from there.

What are bookmarks? ▾

On any entry page, click ☆ Save Entry to bookmark it. Bookmarked entries are collected in the Saved Entries tab of your dashboard for quick access later. You can remove bookmarks at any time.

How do I view my public profile? ▾

Click My Profile in your dashboard sidebar, or visit /profile.php?username=yourname. Your profile is public and shows all your approved entries and your contribution stats.
